# Code of Conduct

**Date:** 21 November 2025  
**Version:** 1.0

This Code of Conduct is based on the Contributor Covenant, version 1.0.

---

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make the Authverse community welcoming, safe, and equitable for all.

We commit to fostering an environment that respects the dignity, rights, and contributions of all individuals, regardless of characteristics such as race, ethnicity, caste, color, age, physical appearance, neurodiversity, disability, sex or gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, language, philosophy or religion, national or social origin, socio-economic status, education level, or any other status.  
Every participant who engages in good faith is entitled to the same protections and privileges.

---

## Encouraged Behaviors

To maintain a healthy, inclusive community, we agree to uphold the following standards:

- Respect the purpose of our community, our activities, and our shared spaces.
- Engage kindly, honestly, and constructively with others.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Take responsibility for our actions and contributions.
- Offer and accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Commit to repairing harm when it occurs.
- Act in ways that promote the well-being and long-term health of the community.

---

## Restricted Behaviors

The following behaviors are prohibited. Any instance, threat, or promotion of these behaviors constitutes a violation of this Code of Conduct:

- **Harassment:** Ignoring boundaries or giving unwanted personal attention after being asked to stop.
- **Personal attacks:** Insulting or demeaning comments toward community members or groups.
- **Stereotyping or discrimination:** Making assumptions about a personâ€™s personality or behavior based on identity traits.
- **Sexualization:** Engaging in behavior that would be considered inappropriately intimate within the context of the community.
- **Violation of confidentiality:** Sharing private or personal information without permission.
- **Endangerment:** Encouraging, threatening, or causing violence or harm.
- Any behavior that threatens the safety or well-being of the community.

---

## Other Restrictions

- **Misleading identity:** Impersonating someone else or attempting to evade enforcement actions.
- **Failure to credit sources:** Not properly attributing content or ideas.
- **Promotional materials:** Sharing commercial or marketing content outside accepted norms.
- **Irresponsible communication:** Sharing content that includes or promotes restricted behaviors.

---

## Reporting an Issue

If you witness or experience a violation, please report it as soon as possible.

**Report violations to:** **info@authverse.abdirahmandev.com**

Community Moderators take all reports seriously and will respond promptly. They will investigate reports through message logs, discussions, or interviews when necessary.  
Moderators strive to remain transparent while prioritizing participant safety and confidentiality. Enforcement actions are handled privately unless all parties agree otherwise.

---

## Addressing and Repairing Harm

When a violation is confirmed, Moderators may use the following enforcement ladder. Depending on severity, steps may be skipped.

### 1. Warning

**Event:** Minor or first-time violation.  
**Consequence:** Private written warning.  
**Repair:** Apology, acknowledgment, clarification of expectations.

### 2. Temporarily Limited Activities

**Event:** Repeated or more serious violation.  
**Consequence:** Temporary restriction from certain community spaces or interactions.  
**Repair:** Reflection period, renewed commitment, thoughtful reintegration.

### 3. Temporary Suspension

**Event:** Significant harm or repeated violations.  
**Consequence:** Temporary removal from the community with conditions for return.  
**Repair:** Compliance with conditions, demonstrated improvement.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Event:** Severe or sustained harmful behavior.  
**Consequence:** Permanent removal from all community spaces.  
**Repair:** None; used only when other remedies have failed or safety cannot be ensured.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Authverse community spaces, both online and offline, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, social media, and events.  
It also applies when individuals represent the project in any public context.
